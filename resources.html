Menstrual Policy for Workplace

  • Menstrual Leave: Allow flexible working hours or additional leave options for women experiencing discomfort during their menstrual cycle.
  • Accessibility: Ensure easy access to sanitary products within the workplace, and consider providing them for free.
  • Privacy and Comfort: Provide a comfortable and private space for women to manage menstrual needs, including proper disposal facilities.
  • Education and Awareness: Conduct workshops to raise awareness about menstrual health, breaking stigma, and fostering a supportive work environment.
  • Flexible Work Arrangements: Allow flexible work hours or remote work during the menstrual cycle, recognizing individual needs and productivity.
  • Healthcare Support: Offer health insurance that covers gynecological check-ups and treatments related to menstrual health.
  • Open Communication: Encourage open communication about menstrual health needs, creating a supportive atmosphere for discussions.

Menopause Policy for Workplace

  • Flexible Work Arrangements: Allow flexibility to accommodate symptoms like hot flashes and fatigue.
  • Education and Awareness: Provide resources to educate employees on menopause, reducing stigma, and fostering understanding.
  • Healthcare Support: Include menopause-related healthcare coverage in insurance plans.
  • Supportive Environment: Create a supportive atmosphere encouraging open communication about menopause challenges and needs.
  • Wellness Programs: Offer wellness initiatives addressing physical and mental well-being during menopause.
